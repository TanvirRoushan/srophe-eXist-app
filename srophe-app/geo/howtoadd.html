Instructions for Contributors to The Syriac Gazetteer

Thank you for your interest in improving the Syriac Gazetteer! The Syriac Gazetteer belongs to the community of users, and all users benefit from the additional information submitted by others. There are several ways to add data to the Gazetteer:

  • Notify us of a mistake
  • Add data to an existing entry
  • Create a new entry
  • Contribute data pertaining to several records simultaneously

In each case, you will be asked to submit information using a web form and specifying the sources for the information. Your submission will then be sent to an editor for review, because Syriaca.org is committed to maintaining the highest standards of scholarly integrity. Complete information in your submission (including full citations of relevant sources and your contact information for any follow-up questions) will expedite the editorial process, although the editorsâ€™ work is on a volunteer basis and has no fixed timeline. Failure to include complete citations may result in editors choosing not to approve a submission. When the editor approves a submission, it becomes part of the Gazetteer citing your intervention. In order to contribute, we ask you to submit new data under a Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported License (http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/), the same license under which the entire Syriac Gazetteer is published, so that your contribution can be included in the relevant entries.

Notify us of a mistake

Using the following link, please inform us which page URI the mistake is on, where on the page the mistake occurs, and a citation for the correct information (except in the case of obviously misspelled words). Please also include your email address, so that we can follow up with you regarding anything which is unclear. The form to notify us of an error is here.

Add data to an existing entry

If there is information which you would like to add to an existing place entry in the Syriac Gazetteer, please use the link below to inform us about the information, your (primary or scholarly) source for the information, and your contact information so that we can credit you for the modification. For categories of information which the Syriac Gazetteer structure can support, please see the section headings on the entry for Edessa and specify in your submission which category or categories this new information falls into. At present this information should be entered into the email form here, although there is an additional delay in this process as the data needs to be encoded in the appropriate structured data format and assigned a URI. A structured form for submitting new entries is under development.

Create a new entry

If there is a place which is relevant to Syriac Studies but does not yet have an entry in the Syriac Gazetteer, please let us know. Please use the following link to inform us what you know about the place in question, including full citations to the source of the information. At a bare minimum, a place record can be created for a place based on a name (in English or Syriac), a source using that name (whether a primary source or a work of scholarship), and a place type. Your name is also necessary in order to credit you as the creator of the entry. Additional information would also be useful, such as additional names, location (in prose or coordinates), attestations in primary sources, known religious communities, links to other projects, etc., but it is important that every individual statement have a citation to a primary or scholarly source. At present this information should be entered into the email form here, although there is an additional delay in this process as the data needs to be encoded in the appropriate structured data format and assigned a URI. A structured form for submitting new entries is under development.

Contribute data pertaining to several records simultaneously

The editors welcome the sharing of new datasets with information about several Gazetteer entries or new records to be created. Since each case is unique, it is best to contact the general editors in order to discuss the format your data is currently in and how best to share that data with the Gazetteer.
